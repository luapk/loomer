# LEO AND THE DOLPHIN

A 76-second wordless short film for AI video generation. 16:9 aspect ratio. 14 shots. No dialogue — sound design and ambient carry the story.

## Narrative arc

A small boy on a windswept pier loses his beloved kite to a sudden gust. A bottlenose dolphin recovers it from the waves and tows it back to him. They share one held look across the water before the dolphin disappears, leaving the boy with a story he can never prove. The dramatic question — *will he get the kite back?* — is set in shot 8 (the loss) and answered in shot 11 (the towing). The deeper question — *what just happened to him?* — is answered only in the final image: he understands he has touched a world that doesn't usually show itself. Withholding: the dolphin's existence is concealed until shot 10's dorsal-fin reveal. Visual rhyme: Leo's hands holding the spool (shot 02) bookend with his hands holding the sodden kite (shot 14) — same composition, transformed bond.

## Followability audit

✅ **Establishing logic**: Shot 01 is an EWS that maps the world entirely — pier, sea, sky, scale, the singular crimson against grey. Shot 04 re-establishes Leo at working scale.

✅ **Line of interest**: The vertical axis between Leo and the kite is maintained in Shots 02–07. The horizontal axis between dolphin and pier in Shots 10–13 is consistent. The crossing between the dolphin's incoming direction (Shot 11, screen-right to screen-left) and outgoing direction (Shot 13, screen-left to screen-right) is properly punctuated by the **neutral angle of Shot 12** (camera looking diagonally between two profiled subjects) which permits the safe pivot.

✅ **Screen direction**: Kite consistently upper-screen-right through Shots 01–07 (matching wind from sea direction). Dolphin enters screen-right in Shot 10, tows screen-left in Shot 11, then after the neutral Shot 12 exits screen-right in Shot 13 (back to its sea origin). Leo's eyeline tracks the kite (screen-right and up) in 04–05, the lost kite (screen-right and down) in 09, the dolphin (screen-right and down) in 12, the empty water (screen-right) in 14 — consistent throughout.

✅ **30° rule**: Every adjacent pair of Leo shots differs by at least 30° in angle and/or scale. Shot 05 (CU dolly-in) → Shot 06 (CU static, same framing held briefly) — this pair is the closest call but the action change (the gust) provides motion-cut motivation that makes it read as a moment-shift, not a jump-cut. The visible change in his hair/expression carries the cut.

✅ **Number / scale contrast**: Scales used: EWS (1), low WS (1), WS (3), MS (3), MCU (2), CU (3), ECU (1). Mix achieved. Durations vary 4s/5s/6s/8s. Rhythm built.
